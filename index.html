M.Sc. Thesis Work, Laboratory of Molecular Biology, BSMRSTU, Bangladesh

(July 2023 Nov 2023)

Thesis Title: Cinnamomum tamala Leafs Role in Liver Protection from Overdose Paracetamol-Induced Hepatotoxicity in Swiss Albino Mice Model.

Findings: The Cinnamomum tamala leaf extract was discovered to possess the enhancement of antioxidant capacity with lower NAPQI metabolites in mice and protect the liver from hepatotoxicity, determining this with biomarker analysis. Moreover, histopathological slides showed minimum inflammation, inflammatory cell infiltration, and vascular edematous congestion in hepatocytes. The cytochrome P450 isoforms (CYP2E1, CYP3A4, and CYP1A2) were subjected to site-specific molecular docking with the compounds identified by the GC-MS study and observed two compounds showed the top docking score against CYP2E1 protein. Ultimately, post-dynamics data yielded a satisfactory result for hepatoprotective activity, as this result was also verified by using the Molecular Dynamics Simulation.

Priyanka Paul Resume

My research focuses on isolating bioactive compounds from natural sources, such as plants, fungi, and marine organisms, and evaluating their therapeutic potential. Specifically, my thesis explored the hepatoprotective properties of Cinnamomum tamala leaf extract against paracetamol-induced liver damage. This work contributes to the development of safer, targeted therapeutic solutions for liver diseases, offering insights into antioxidant mechanisms and site-specific molecular interactions.

I use computational tools for tasks such as molecular docking, molecular dynamics simulation, and biomarker analysis. These tools help validate experimental findings, allowing us to predict interactions between bioactive compounds and specific proteins, such as cytochrome P450 isoforms. This integrative approach ensures that our research is both experimentally robust and computationally validated, paving the way for targeted drug development.
